E — The Neural Archipelago

"Islands of intelligence connected by rivers of signal"

Mechanism clusters float as organic nodes in two hemispheres — left for training, right for inference. A central Galton board spine connects them, probability cascading down the center. Corpus callosum bridges cross weight-sharing between hemispheres.

Each node is a self-contained module (gears, cams, logic gates inside) mounted on curved brass arms extending from the spine. Dendrite tendrils extend outward — some structural, some carry secondary signals. The whole structure reads as a neural network diagram made physical.

During Act 1 (Training), the left hemisphere is alive — weights updating, gradients flowing, error converging, chime ringing. During Act 2 (Inference), activity shifts right — tokens enter, attention expands, hidden state forms, confidence iris opens.

  • Clearest two-act narrative (left active → right active)
  • Each node independently designable and serviceable
  • Galton board spine is the visual anchor
  • Organic shape reads as "brain" from any angle
  • ~1.5m wide, ~1.2m tall, ~0.5m deep
  • Many curved support arms needed (fabrication complexity)
F

F — The Thinking Machine

"A brain you can see thinking"

The outer silhouette IS a brain — an organic, asymmetric outline with sulci (folds) dividing regions. Mechanism clusters sit inside the brain regions like functional areas of a real cortex. Frontal lobe = memory/planning. Parietal = computation. Temporal = processing. Occipital = output.

The brain silhouette is formed by a thin organic frame (bent brass tubing or 3D-printed organic lattice). Interior is open — you see through the "skull" to the mechanisms inside. The Galton board drops below as the brainstem.

Two hemispheres map to two acts. The organic frame gives it personality — it's not a machine, it's a thinking being.

  • Instantly recognizable as "brain" — no explanation needed
  • Brain region mapping is scientifically resonant
  • Organic frame = dramatic visual signature
  • Brainstem Galton board is anatomically correct
  • ~1.2m wide, ~1.0m tall brain + 0.4m brainstem
  • Organic frame harder to fabricate than straight supports
  • Brain shape constrains mechanism placement

G — The Synaptic Web

"A neuron's-eye view — you ARE inside the network"

A large central hub (the core computation: ALU, logic gates, Galton board) with 8 radial arms extending outward like axons. Each arm terminates in a mechanism node. Secondary tendrils branch off each node like dendrites.

The topology is a star graph — everything connects through the center. This mirrors how a single neuron connects: inputs arrive on dendrites (left arms), outputs leave on axons (right arms). The viewer stands at the center of a neuron's perspective.

Training mechanisms cluster on left arms, inference on right. The Galton board sits inside the central hub — probability cascading through the heart of the network.

  • Radial symmetry is inherently dramatic and balanced
  • Central hub naturally houses the Galton board + ALU
  • Arms can be different lengths (hierarchy = importance)
  • Viewable from 360 degrees — no "back"
  • Scalable: add arms for new mechanisms
  • ~1.5m diameter radial spread, ~1.0m tall
  • Central hub can become crowded
  • Less obviously "brain-shaped" — more abstract
