Verdict: Proven but Quiet
Every mechanism is validated from Karanika. Computation is mathematically exact. But motion is predominantly subtle — small rotations, gentle displacements. The pantographs breathe, the strings glint, but the worm gears and rack-pinions are invisible precision. Gallery visitors need to lean in close to see computation happening.

Verdict: Dramatic but Complex
Every computation is VISIBLE as physical bit-flips. The carry cascade is genuinely theatrical — a ripple of snaps propagating through the adder. But: 4-bit weights mean only 16 possible values per weight (very coarse). Neural networks struggle with <4-bit quantization. Training convergence becomes unreliable. Also: 600-900 precision parts is a fabrication marathon. Each logic gate needs exact lever geometry or the binary state becomes ambiguous.

Verdict: Best of Both Worlds
The hub CLICKS and SNAPS (digital). The arms BREATHE and FLOW (analog). Two contrasting textures of motion in one sculpture. The Babbage carry cascade + logic gates give the center a mechanical typewriter energy. The pantographs + wave field give the periphery an organic, living quality. DAC/ADC converters at the boundary are themselves a teaching moment: "this is where digital becomes analog." The 4-bit limitation is mitigated by the analog arms doing fine-grained gradient work — the ALU handles integer arithmetic, the spiral cams handle continuous multiplication.
