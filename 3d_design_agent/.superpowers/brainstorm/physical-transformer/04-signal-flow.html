The narrative arc: A viewer first watches training mode for 5-10 minutes — levers swinging, worms clicking, whippletree settling. Then they switch to PREDICT, set a novel input, and watch the machine correctly predict the answer to something it's never seen. That moment — "wait, it actually learned?" — is the entire point of the sculpture.

The escapement controls all timing. Target: ~1 second per tick, adjustable by changing the balance wheel inertia.

THE TRANSPARENT MIDPLANE

All signal routing between layers happens through a clear acrylic midplane. 1.5mm brass rods pass through precision-drilled holes in the acrylic sheet. From the front, you see golden lines cutting through crystal-clear plastic — the "nerves" of the machine, carrying information between the neurons.

The midplane is both structural (it holds the layers at fixed spacing) and aesthetic (it makes the invisible visible). Every signal path in the network has a physical brass rod you can point to and say: "that carries the activation from hidden neuron 2 to output neuron 1."

Rod count: 9 rods (input → hidden routing) + 3 rods (hidden → output activations) + 3 rods (output → comparison) + 6 rods (error backprop) = ~21 brass rods through the midplane. Each one a visible, traceable signal line.
