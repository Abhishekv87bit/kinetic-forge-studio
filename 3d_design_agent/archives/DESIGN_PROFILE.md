# Design Profile: Kinetic Sculpture Aesthetic

## Primary Influence: Reuben Margolin

> "The analog has the elegance and embedded meaning of an analytical solution."
> "Viewers report feeling the sculpture resonating in their chest."

### What This Means
- Motion derived from **physical geometry**: cams, levers, helices, pulleys
- **Single motor → many synchronized outputs**
- Mathematical precision creating sensory/emotional response
- No digital controllers - pure mechanical logic

---

## Target Emotion: Meditative Calm

### The Feel
- Slow tempo preferred
- Hypnotic repetition
- "Could watch for hours"
- Breathing, not ticking

### Key Qualities
| Quality | Description |
|---------|-------------|
| **Continuous** | No abrupt state changes. Motion flows. |
| **Phase-offset** | Elements offset in time create rolling waves |
| **Uniform** | Adjacent elements move in harmony, not independently |
| **Sinusoidal** | Mathematical curves, not linear interpolation |
| **Redundant** | Many elements doing similar things = hypnotic |

---

## Motion Vocabulary

*Terms I use and what they mean:*

| Term | Meaning | Mechanism Match |
|------|---------|-----------------|
| "Breathing" | Slow inhale/exhale (3-4 sec cycle) | Scotch yoke (pure sine) |
| "Rolling" | Phase-offset wave across elements | Offset cranks on shaft |
| "Swelling" | Multiple elements rising/falling together | Common driver, sync |
| "Languid" | Deliberately slow, never rushed | Low RPM, high reduction |
| "Ripple" | Small amplitude, high frequency | Eccentric cam chain |

---

## Mechanism Preferences

### USE
| For This Effect | Use This Mechanism |
|-----------------|-------------------|
| Continuous rotation | Helical cam or eccentric |
| Wave propagation | Phase-offset cranks on common shaft |
| Synchronized ripple | Pulley/string network or linkage cascade |
| Breathing feel | Scotch yoke (pure sinusoidal output) |
| Variable speed | Nautilus/log spiral gear |

### AVOID
- **Geneva drives** - jerky indexing breaks the meditation
- **Ratchets** - click motion is jarring
- **Independent motors** - breaks synchronization, feels chaotic
- **Linear motion without easing** - robotic, not organic

---

## Anti-References (What I DON'T Want)

- Jerky, mechanical feel
- Cartoon-like bouncing (too playful)
- Overly complex multi-motor systems
- Fast, aggressive motion
- "Busy" - too many competing motions
- Novelty/gimmick mechanisms

## How We Work Together

### Before Code
1. I describe what I want (feeling, motion quality)
2. Claude asks clarifying questions about **feel**, not just function
3. Claude requests visual references if helpful
4. Claude offers 2-3 mechanism choices with aesthetic tradeoffs
5. We iterate on feel until aligned

### Code Happens Only After
- We've agreed on motion quality
- We've agreed on tempo/timing
- We've agreed on mechanism approach

### Validation Is Implicit
Claude will automatically:
- Verify Grashof condition
- Check coupler lengths at 4 positions (0°, 90°, 180°, 270°)
- Ensure no collisions
- Calculate forces and torques

No checklists needed. The math happens behind the scenes.

## Quick Test

*If you're unsure whether a design matches this profile, ask:*

1. Could I watch this for 10 minutes without getting bored or agitated?
2. Does the motion feel inevitable (mathematical) or arbitrary?
3. Is there a clear relationship between elements, or are they independent?
4. Does it breathe, or does it tick?

If the answers are Yes, Yes, Related, Breathe → it matches the profile.
