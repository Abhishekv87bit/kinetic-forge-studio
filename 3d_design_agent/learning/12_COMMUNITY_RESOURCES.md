## 16. PS70 Harvard Digital Fabrication

**Course Site:** [nathanmelenbrink.github.io/ps70](https://nathanmelenbrink.github.io/ps70/)
**Instructor:** Nathan Melenbrink (Harvard)

### Week 3 Assignment: Kinetic Sculptures

Requirements:
- Design and create a kinetic sculpture
- Use at least two different materials
- Incorporate at least one static joint
- Incorporate at least one prismatic joint

### Mechanisms Taught

| Mechanism | Application |
|-----------|-------------|
| **Geneva Drive** | Intermittent rotary motion with indexed/locked positions |
| **Spur Gear Systems** | Teeth count, module calculations, pitch diameter, backlash |
| **Four-Bar Linkages** | Constraining motion to specific paths |
| **Gear Ratios** | Prime number teeth for wear distribution |

### Student Projects to Study (With Files!)

1. **Geneva Drive Sculpture** (Tom Kinsley) -- geneva_drive_v9.f3d available
   - Process: Laser-cut cardboard -> Fusion 360 -> Final fabrication

2. **Genesis Clock** (Fouzia Raza) -- Large spur gears (60 teeth, 120mm pitch)
   - Inspired by Clayton Boyer's mechanical clocks

3. **Three Tiny Dancers** (Kassia Love) -- 12-tooth + 6-tooth satellites
   - Key learning: Fusion360 animation with As-Built Joints

## Remember

**Don't reinvent. Remix.**

Every mechanism you need has probably been animated, simulated, or built by someone. Your job is to find it, understand it, and apply it to your vision.
