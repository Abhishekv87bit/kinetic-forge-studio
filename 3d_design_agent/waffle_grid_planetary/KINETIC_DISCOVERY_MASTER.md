## HOW KINETIC ARTISTS DISCOVER THEIR NICHE

### The Universal Arc
```
SEED (observation that won't let go)
  → OBSESSION (can't stop thinking about it)
    → ACCIDENT (something unexpected redirects you)
      → REFRAME (the idea transforms into something bigger)
        → TECHNIQUE (mechanism serves the reframed idea — picked LAST, not first)
```

### Artist Case Studies

| Artist | Seed | Accident | Reframe | Technique | Territory OWNED |
|--------|------|----------|---------|-----------|-----------------|
| **Reuben Margolin** | Caterpillar motion on Utah hike | Ground ruined smooth motion → looked UP, suspended | "I've always been making waves" | Cam → cable → pulley (Kelvin) | Wave superposition sculpture |
| **Theo Jansen** | Newspaper fantasy: beach skeletons building dunes | Forgot about saving Holland → evolution became the art | Life creation, not engineering | Genetic algorithm linkage optimization | Evolutionary walking machines |
| **Alexander Calder** | Wire circus, childhood toys | Removed motors → air currents drove sculpture better | Invisible force, balance as art | Gravity + air (NO mechanism) | Balanced mobiles |
| **Jean Tinguely** | Motor on object at age 12 | Drawings were boring → machine was interesting | Process IS the art, product is waste | Deliberately broken motors + junk | Anti-machine chaos |
| **John Edmark** | Fibonacci spirals in plants | Stroboscope revealed hidden rotation in 3D prints | Hidden math made visible | 3D printed golden ratio + strobe | Stroboscopic geometric illusion |
| **William Darrell** | Biological organisms — worms, flowers | Uncanny valley between alive and mechanical | Mechanically abstracted organisms | Single motor → gear/belt/cam tree, 3D printed | Bio-mechanical uncanny valley |

### Key Insight
Your niche won't come from a list. It'll come from running experiments, seeing something unexpected, and going "wait... what if..."
